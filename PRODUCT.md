# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are solo founders, indie hackers, technical founders, and early startup teams validating an idea before they commit to building. They use Thesys while navigating ambiguous strategic work: assessing market viability, choosing a customer segment, identifying competitors, exposing kill-risk assumptions, planning validation experiments, and recording decisions.

Later users include product managers, consultants, venture studios, innovation teams, product marketing and competitive intelligence teams, and investors who need durable evidence, repeatable workflows, and decision traceability across strategic projects.

## Product Purpose

Thesys is an AI-native strategic intelligence workspace that turns rough business ideas into evolving, evidence-backed strategic projects. The product should not behave like a chatbot, pitch-deck generator, or startup idea toy. Its core primitive is a persistent project graph: idea, thesis, segments, competitors, evidence, assumptions, experiments, decisions, artifacts, watchlists, and alerts.

Success means users can see the current strategic verdict, the evidence behind it, what remains uncertain, and the next validation step. The product wins when users trust it to remember their strategy, track their evidence, watch how the market changes, and preserve why important decisions were made.

## Brand Personality

Thesys should feel like an expert command center: rigorous, evidence-first, calm, compact, and built for repeated strategic work. The interface should project strategic confidence without decorative theatrics. It should help users move from ambiguity to a clear next action while keeping uncertainty visible.

The voice should be specific, plainspoken, and advisor-like. It should name the verdict, supporting evidence, open questions, and action path directly.

## Anti-references

Avoid positioning or interface patterns that make Thesys feel like:

- a generic AI chatbot
- an AI cofounder persona
- a startup idea generator
- a business plan writer
- a pitch deck generator
- a generated-object workspace with long walls of undifferentiated AI output
- an admin list that hides strategic verdicts behind object counts
- a decorative SaaS landing page where polish outruns evidence

Do not obscure uncertainty with confident-sounding copy. Do not use unexplained confidence percentages as primary UX language. Do not make process inspection compete with conclusions.

## Design Principles

Evidence before advice: every strategic recommendation should make its basis visible through sources, citations, trust checks, or explicit open questions.

Verdict, then next action: primary screens should answer the user's immediate question first, then show what to do next and why it matters.

Persistent strategy memory: the project should feel like a living strategic record, not a sequence of disposable generations.

Expose uncertainty clearly: assumptions, risks, missing evidence, and validation status should be first-class, legible objects.

Compact command-center density: favor scannable rows, tabs, status language, and progressive disclosure over large empty dashboards or marketing-style sections.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text, muted text, placeholders, states, and controls must meet contrast requirements in both dark and light themes. Interactive elements need keyboard access, visible focus states, and labels that do not rely on color alone. Motion should be restrained, state-driven, and compatible with reduced-motion preferences.
